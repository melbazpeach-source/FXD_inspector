# NZ Smoke Alarm Rules — Residential Tenancies (Smoke Alarms and Insulation) Regulations 2016

## Mandatory Requirements

### Placement
- Within **3 metres** of each bedroom door, OR in every room where a person sleeps
- On **each level/storey** of a multi-storey home
- In all rental homes, boarding houses, rental caravans, and self-contained sleep-outs

### Alarm Type (new alarms installed after 1 July 2016)
- Must be **photoelectric** (not ionisation)
- Battery life of **at least 8 years**, OR hard-wired to mains power
- Installed according to manufacturer's instructions
- Must meet international standards (AS 3786:2014 or equivalent)

### Existing Alarms
- Do NOT need to be replaced if they are working and have not passed their expiry date
- When replaced, the new alarm must meet the current standards (photoelectric, long-life)

## Responsibilities

### Landlord must ensure:
- Alarms are working at the **start of each new tenancy**
- Alarms remain in working order during the tenancy
- Replace batteries in common areas of boarding houses

### Tenant must:
- NOT damage, remove, or disconnect a smoke alarm
- Replace dead batteries during the tenancy (older-style alarms with replaceable batteries)
- Notify landlord of any problems as soon as possible

## Penalties
- Landlord non-compliance: up to **$7,200**
- Tenant non-compliance: up to **$4,000**

## Compliance Checklist (per property)

For each alarm, document:
1. Location (e.g., "Hallway outside master bedroom", "Upstairs landing")
2. Type: Photoelectric | Ionisation | Hard-wired | Unknown
3. Power source: Long-life battery (8yr+) | Replaceable battery | Hard-wired
4. Working status: Yes | No | Not tested
5. Expiry date (if visible on unit)
6. Distance from nearest bedroom door (must be ≤ 3m)
7. Installed per manufacturer instructions: Yes | No
8. Meets NZ standards: Yes | No | Unknown

## Compliance Status Logic

COMPLIANT if ALL of the following:
- At least one alarm within 3m of each bedroom
- Each level of multi-storey has at least one alarm
- All alarms are working
- All alarms are photoelectric (or existing non-photoelectric that pre-dates July 2016 and is still working/not expired)
- All alarms have long-life battery (8yr+) or are hard-wired (or are existing older-style still working)

NON-COMPLIANT if ANY of:
- Missing coverage (no alarm within 3m of a bedroom)
- Any alarm not working
- Any alarm past expiry date
- New alarm installed that is not photoelectric
- New alarm installed without long-life battery or hard-wiring
- Missing coverage on a level of multi-storey property

## Best Practice (FENZ Recommendations)
- Interconnected alarms (when one sounds, all sound) — strongly recommended for multi-storey
- Alarm in every bedroom (not just hallway) for maximum protection
- Test alarms monthly
- Clean alarms every 6 months
- Replace all alarms every 10 years regardless of battery status
