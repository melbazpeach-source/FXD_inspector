# NZ Healthy Homes Standards — Compliance Assessment Reference

## Overview
All NZ rental properties must comply with the Healthy Homes Standards (Residential Tenancies (Healthy Homes Standards) Regulations 2019).
- **Mandatory from 1 July 2024** for all rental properties
- **Compliance deadline**: New tenancies must comply within 90 days of tenancy start
- Non-compliance can result in significant financial penalties

## The Five Standards

### 1. HEATING STANDARD
**Requirement**: The main living area must have a fixed heater that can heat the room to at least 18°C.

**Acceptable heaters**: Heat pumps, wood burners, pellet burners, flued gas heaters, electric heaters (fixed), diesel heaters
**NOT acceptable**: Portable heaters, unflued gas heaters, open fireplaces (unless meeting specific criteria)

**Heating capacity calculation**:
- Use the Tenancy Services Heating Assessment Tool OR
- Use the formula from Schedule 2 of the Regulations
- Modern dwelling formula (built to 2008 building code, or upgraded insulation/glazing to 2009 standards)
- Standard formula for all other properties
- Minimum typically around 1.5–2.4 kW for small rooms, up to 5.7+ kW for large living areas

**What to document**:
- Type and brand of heater
- Rated heating capacity (kW)
- Whether it meets the capacity requirement (calculated or tool-assessed)
- Room dimensions (length × width × height)
- Insulation type and R-value
- Glazing type (single/double)
- Climate zone (1=Auckland/Northland, 2=Central NI, 3=South Island/Wellington)

### 2. INSULATION STANDARD
**Requirement**: Ceiling and underfloor insulation required in all rental homes.

**R-value minimums by climate zone**:
- Zone 1 (Auckland, Northland): Ceiling R2.9, Underfloor R1.3
- Zone 2 (Central North Island, Hawke's Bay, Taranaki, Manawatu-Whanganui, Wellington): Ceiling R2.9, Underfloor R1.3
- Zone 3 (Nelson, Marlborough, Canterbury, West Coast, Otago, Southland): Ceiling R3.3, Underfloor R1.3

**Exemptions**: 
- Concrete slab floors (underfloor insulation not required)
- Properties where installation is not reasonably practicable
- Ceiling insulation not required if ceiling height < 1.5m

**What to document**:
- Insulation type (bulk/reflective)
- R-value (ceiling and underfloor)
- Installation date
- Whether it meets zone requirements
- Any exemptions claimed

### 3. VENTILATION STANDARD
**Requirement**: Rental homes must have openable windows and extractor fans.

**Specific requirements**:
- Openable windows in living room, dining room, kitchen, and all bedrooms
- Openable area must be at least 5% of the floor area of the room
- Extractor fans required in kitchens (range hood or ceiling fan venting to outside)
- Extractor fans required in bathrooms (ceiling fan venting to outside, or window)
- Fans must be capable of extracting moisture to outside (not just recirculating)

**What to document**:
- Room-by-room window openable area (measured in m²)
- Floor area of each room
- Whether 5% threshold is met
- Kitchen extractor fan: type, venting method, functional status
- Bathroom extractor fan: type, venting method, functional status

### 4. MOISTURE INGRESS AND DRAINAGE STANDARD
**Requirement**: Rental homes must have efficient drainage and gutters, and ground moisture barriers where applicable.

**Specific requirements**:
- Efficient drainage for stormwater, surface water, and groundwater
- Gutters and downpipes must be in good working order
- Subfloor ground moisture barriers required where subfloor space exists and is accessible
- Ground moisture barrier must cover at least 75% of the subfloor ground area

**What to document**:
- Drainage condition (gutters, downpipes, stormwater)
- Evidence of moisture or water ingress
- Subfloor access and moisture barrier presence/condition
- Any drainage issues identified

### 5. DRAUGHT STOPPING STANDARD
**Requirement**: All unreasonable gaps and holes that cause draughts must be blocked.

**Specific requirements**:
- Gaps and holes in walls, ceilings, floors, and around windows/doors must be blocked
- Unused fireplaces must be closed off or chimneys blocked
- Gaps around pipes, cables, and other penetrations must be filled
- Does NOT require blocking gaps that are necessary for ventilation

**What to document**:
- Location of any gaps or holes identified
- Whether unused fireplaces are blocked
- Condition of door and window seals
- Any draught sources identified

## Compliance Statement
Landlords must provide a Healthy Homes compliance statement with every new tenancy agreement, stating:
- Current compliance status for each of the five standards
- Date by which the property will comply (if not yet compliant)
- Details of any exemptions claimed

## Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Landlords can be fined up to $7,200 per breach
- Tenancy Tribunal can order landlords to remedy non-compliance
- Exemplary damages can be awarded to tenants

## Climate Zones (NZ)
- **Zone 1**: Northland, Auckland
- **Zone 2**: Waikato, Bay of Plenty, Gisborne, Hawke's Bay, Taranaki, Manawatu-Whanganui, Wellington, Tasman, Nelson, Marlborough (north)
- **Zone 3**: West Coast, Canterbury, Otago, Southland, Marlborough (south)
